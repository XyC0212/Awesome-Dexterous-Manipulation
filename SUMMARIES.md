# Paper Summaries

One-sentence summaries used for retrieval, newest first. Full metadata — labels, venue,
year, affiliation, and links — lives in the [README table](README.md); your own opinions
and longer notes live one-file-per-paper in [notes/](notes/).

- **TeleDexter: Towards Human-level Dexterous Teleoperation** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11481)) — TeleDexter is a hand-object co-tracking teleoperation controller trained with single-stage RL on consecutive fingertip-and-object subgoals derived from human reference motions (a hybrid sparse-subgoal + dense-tracking reward, with random action masking for zero-shot sim-to-real), reaching 75% average success across seven reorientation and long-horizon tool-use tasks on two dexterous hands where baselines fail, and its collected demonstrations train autonomous behavioral-cloning policies.
- **RynnWorld-Teleop: An Action-Conditioned World Model for Digital Teleoperation** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06558)) — RynnWorld-Teleop replaces physical teleoperation with a generative "digital teleoperation" world model that turns an operator's hand-pose stream plus a single reference image into high-fidelity egocentric robot video and retargetable action labels, via depth-aware skeletal conditioning, progressive human-to-robot training on a video Diffusion Transformer, and streaming autoregressive distillation for 40+ FPS on one H100 — yielding synthetic demos that enable zero-shot sim2real and lift real-data success rates by up to 20%.
- **AnyDexRT: Calibration-Free Dexterous Hand Retargeting with Few-Shot Human Guidance** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08341)) — AnyDexRT is a calibration-free teleoperation retargeting method that maps human to robot hand motion across diverse dexterous hands via self-supervised fingertip-correspondence learning plus few-shot human guidance and a pinch contact classifier, lifting local-motion consistency from 59.8% to 90.2% across 7 hands and cutting real-world teleop task times by up to 63% with markedly higher pinch success.
- **DexVerse: A Modular Benchmark for Multi-Task, Multi-Embodiment Dexterous Manipulation** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08751)) — DexVerse is a modular simulation benchmark of 100 dexterous tasks across 8 skill categories, 3 arms and 6 hands with configurable visual variations, plus a VR (Apple Vision Pro) teleoperation interface and 3,180 multimodal demonstrations, on which representative policies (Diffusion Policy, DP3, OpenVLA, π₀.₅) reach only 34% mean success across 19 tasks — exposing large cross-task and visuomotor generalization gaps.
- **Dex4D: Task-Agnostic Point Track Policy for Sim-to-Real Dexterous Manipulation** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.15828)) — Dex4D trains a single anypose-to-anypose, 3D-point-track-conditioned dexterous policy (a PPO teacher distilled via DAgger into a transformer action world model with paired-point encoding) and drives it at test time with point tracks lifted from generated-video task plans, reaching 60% sim success and zero-shot real-world transfer (19/40 on unseen tasks) without finetuning.
- **Play2Perfect: What Matters in Dexterous Play Pretraining for Precise Assembly?** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26428)) — Play2Perfect pretrains a goal-conditioned RL policy through task-agnostic "play" (grasping, in-hand reorientation, and 6D pose-reaching on diverse objects) then finetunes it on sparse-reward CAD-derived assembly, yielding a manipulation prior 33× more sample-efficient than RL from scratch and zero-shot sim-to-real success on 0.5 mm-clearance insertions (60%), long-horizon multi-part assembly, and screwing.
- **DexJoCo: A Benchmark and Toolkit for Task-Oriented Dexterous Manipulation on MuJoCo** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.16257)) — DexJoCo is a MuJoCo benchmark of 11 functionally grounded dexterous-hand tasks (tool-use, bimanual, long-horizon, reasoning) paired with a ~$2,300 glove-and-tracker teleoperation rig and GeoRT retargeting to collect 1.1K demonstrations, and shows five modern IL/VLA policies (ACT, Diffusion Policy, π₀.₅, GR00T N1.5) degrade sharply under visual randomization and fail at genuine language conditioning.
- **CHORD: Learning Dexterous Manipulation Using Contact Wrench Guidance from Human Demonstration** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00033)) — CHORD guides RL with an object-centric contact-wrench-space reward that matches the forces and torques a human demonstration induces on objects (rather than 3D contact positions), reaching an average 82.12% success across 1,831 bimanual rigid/articulated tasks, beating DexMachina/ManipTrans/SPIDER, and transferring to a real Dexmate robot with Sharpa hands.
- **HumanEgo: Zero-Shot Robot Learning from Minutes of Human Egocentric Videos** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24934)) — HumanEgo lifts human egocentric videos into entity-level hand-object interaction representations and trains a flow-matching policy with dense auxiliary objectives, reaching 92.5% success on four real-world tasks from just 30 minutes of human video per task and transferring zero-shot to novel robots, cameras, and environments.
- **Do as I Do: Dexterous Manipulation Data from Everyday Human Videos** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19333)) — Do as I Do reconstructs hand-object interactions from in-the-wild egocentric and exocentric RGB videos and retargets them to multi-fingered robot hands, setting a new state of the art in extracting dexterous manipulation trajectories from everyday video.
- **T-Rex: Tactile-Reactive Dexterous Manipulation** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17055)) — T-Rex pairs a 100-hour tactile dataset with a variable-rate Mixture-of-Transformers and a temporal tactile encoder to learn tactile-reactive dexterous policies, beating the strongest baseline by over 30% on twelve precision force-control and deformable-object tasks.
- **EgoEngine: From Egocentric Human Videos to High-Fidelity Dexterous Robot Demonstrations** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12604)) — EgoEngine builds a digital twin from a single egocentric human video and jointly generates a high-fidelity robot observation video plus executable action trajectories, enabling the first zero-shot visuomotor dexterous policy learning from human videos without any real-robot demonstrations.
- **Video2Sim2Real: Full-Stack Autonomous Dexterous Skill Acquisition from a Single Human Video** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08828)) — Video2Sim2Real reconstructs a simulator-ready digital twin from a single human video, then pairs imitation learning (recalibrating robot configurations from noisy real point clouds) with residual RL for finger-level adaptation to autonomously acquire dexterous skills with strong sim-to-real transfer.
- **SimToolReal: An Object-Centric Policy for Zero-Shot Dexterous Tool Manipulation** ([paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.16863)) — SimToolReal trains a single object-centric RL policy over procedurally generated tool-like objects in simulation to achieve zero-shot dexterous tool use on a real 22-DoF five-fingered hand, outperforming retargeting and fixed-grasp baselines by 37% across 120 real-world rollouts.
